Ceviche

A refreshing and tangy dish made with raw fish marinated in citrus juices, typically lime, and mixed with onions, cilantro, and chili peppers. It's a popular appetizer in coastal regions, offering a zesty and fresh flavor.

Ingredients

  • Fresh fish (commonly white fish like sea bass or tilapia)
  • Lime juice (or lemon juice)
  • Red onion (sliced)
  • Cilantro (chopped)
  • Chili peppers (like jalape√±o or aji amarillo)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Optional: Corn, sweet potatoes, or avocado for garnish

Preparation

  1. Cut the fish into small, bite-sized pieces.
  2. Place the fish in a bowl and cover with lime (or lemon) juice. Ensure the fish is fully submerged.
  3. Let it marinate for 10-15 minutes, allowing the acid to "cook" the fish.
  4. While the fish is marinating, slice the onions, chop the cilantro, and deseed and chop the chili peppers.
  5. Add the onions, cilantro, and chili peppers to the fish mixture.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve chilled with optional garnishes like corn, sweet potatoes, or avocado.
