Lomo saltado

A flavorful Peruvian stir-fry that combines tender strips of beef with onions, tomatoes, and peppers, all sautéed in a soy sauce-based marinade. It's traditionally served with rice and fried potatoes, blending Asian and Latin American influences.

Ingredients

  • Beef (typically sirloin or tenderloin, cut into strips)
  • Red onions (sliced)
  • Tomatoes (sliced)
  • Yellow chili peppers (aji amarillo, or a substitute)
  • Soy sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Cilantro (chopped)
  • French fries (for garnish)
  • Cooked white rice

Preparation

  1. Heat a large pan or wok over medium-high heat and add some oil.
  2. Add the beef strips and sear until browned on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, sauté onions, garlic, and chili peppers for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add the tomatoes and cook for another minute or two, keeping the vegetables slightly crisp.
  5. Return the beef to the pan and add soy sauce and vinegar. Stir everything to combine.
  6. Toss in cilantro and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  7. Serve the lomo saltado over a bed of rice and top with French fries.
